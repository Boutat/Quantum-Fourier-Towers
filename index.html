Hierarchy of Solvable Groups

$$ \{e\}=G_0 \triangleleft G_1 \triangleleft \cdots \triangleleft G_n=G. $$ $$ Q_i = G_{i+1}/G_i. $$

Example: S₃

$$ \{e\} \triangleleft A_3 \triangleleft S_3. $$ $$ A_3\cong \mathbb Z_3, \qquad S_3/A_3\cong \mathbb Z_2. $$

The nontrivial quotient layer acts on the Fourier modes of the lower layer through conjugation.

Hadamard Identity

$$ HXH = Z. $$

The Fourier Tower construction may be viewed as a hierarchical generalization of this elementary identity. The Hadamard transform converts translation operators into phase operators. The Fourier Tower extends this principle to multiple interacting quotient layers.
